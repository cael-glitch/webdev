Who were the signers

Famous signers

Some of the most famous signers

John Hancock
A merchant, statesman, and prominent Patriot of the American Revolution. As president of the Second Continental Congress, he was the first signer of the Declaration of Independence. His large, flamboyant signature has made his name an informal synonym for "signature".
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath who was a leading author, printer, political theorist, politician, postmaster, scientist, inventor, and diplomat. He was the oldest signer of the Declaration of Independence at age 70 and also signed the U.S. Constitution.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence and the third President of the United States. His words articulated the ideals of American independence and individual liberty.
Elizabeth Cady Stanton
A leading figure in the early women's rights movement. She was a primary organizer of the Seneca Falls Convention in 1848 and the main author of the Declaration of Sentiments, which called for social and legal equality for women.
Frederick Douglass
An abolitionist and social reformer who escaped from slavery. He attended and signed the Declaration of Sentiments at the Seneca Falls Convention, arguing eloquently for women's suffrage.
George Clymer
A merchant and politician who signed both the Declaration of Independence and the U.S. Constitution, a distinction shared by only a handful of individuals.
George Washington
Commander-in-chief of the Continental Army during the American Revolution and the first U.S. President. He was the presiding officer of the Constitutional Convention and signed the U.S. Constitution.
James Madison
Known as the "Fatrher of the Constitution," he drafted much of the U.S. Constitution and the Bill of Rights. He later became the fourth President of the United States.

The most famous signer is John Hancock, whose large signature became a synonym for "signature". Other famous signers include Thomas Jefferson, Benjamin Franklin, and John Adams.
