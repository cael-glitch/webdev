National League West Teams

The National League West division was created in 1969. It includes the Arizona Diamondbacks, Colorado Rockies, Los Angeles Dodgers, San Diego Padres, and San Francisco Giants. Notable former members include the Atlanta Braves and Houston Astros. The division is known for the rivalry between the Dodgers and Giants, and its teams have won multiple World Series titles.

Arizona Diamondbacks

The Arizona Diamondbacks joined in 1998, winning the World Series in 2001. They play at Chase Field. Their original colors represented Arizona's environment and local sports, later changing to reflect the state's landscape. Their rival is the Los Angeles Dodgers. A key player is Corbin Carroll.

Colorado Rockies

The Colorado Rockies joined in 1993. They play at Coors Field, where some seats are one mile above sea level. Their colors are purple, black, and silver. They won the NL pennant in 2007. A key player is Brenton Doyle.

Los Angeles Dodgers

A founding member of the NL West in 1969, the Dodgers moved from Brooklyn to Los Angeles in 1958. They play at Dodger Stadium. They are the most successful team in the division, with multiple World Series titles, including in 2020. Their colors are Dodger blue and white. They have a notable rivalry with the San Francisco Giants. Key players include Mookie Betts.

San Diego Padres

The Padres joined the NL West as an expansion team in 1969. They play at Petco Park in downtown San Diego. San Francisco Giants

The Giants are a founding member of the NL West. Originating as the New York Gothams in 1883, the team moved to San Francisco in 1958. They play at Oracle Park and share a historic rivalry with the Dodgers. The Giants won three World Series titles in a five-year span during the 2010s. Their team colors are black and orange. Notable players include Willie Mays, Madison Bumgarner, and Buster Posey.
