National League Central Teams

The National League Central division was created in 1994 as part of a league-wide realignment. It was originally composed of five teams from the NL West (Reds, Astros) and NL East (Cubs, Pirates, Cardinals). The Milwaukee Brewers joined the NL Central in 1998, moving from the AL Central. Houston left for the AL West in 2013. The division is known for strong rivalries, including the Cubs-Cardinals and Brewers-Cubs matchups.

Chicago Cubs

The Cubs are a charter member of the National League, founded in 1876 as the White Stockings. They have played in Wrigley Field since 1916. Their team colors are royal blue, red, and white. They famously broke a 108-year World Series drought in 2016. Notable players include Ernie Banks, Ron Santo, Ryne Sandberg, and Anthony Rizzo.

Cincinnati Reds

The Cincinnati Reds' history connects to the 1869 Red Stockings, baseball's first professional team. They have won five World Series titles, including back-to-back championships in 1975 and 1976 with the "Big Red Machine." They play at Great American Ball Park. Their colors are red and white. Notable players include Johnny Bench, Joe Morgan, and Pete Rose.

Milwaukee Brewers

The Brewers began in 1969 as the Seattle Pilots and moved to Milwaukee for the 1970 season. They have played in the AL West, AL East, and AL Central before joining the NL Central in 1998. They play at American Family Field. Their colors are navy blue, yellow, and royal blue. Key players include Robin Yount, Paul Molitor, and Christian Yelich.

Pittsburgh Pirates

The Pirates, who joined the National League in 1887, have won five World Series titles.
