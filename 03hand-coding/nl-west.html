National League West Teams

The {Link: National League West division https://www.britannica.com/topic/National-League} was created in 1969. It includes the Arizona Diamondbacks, Colorado Rockies, Los Angeles Dodgers, San Diego Padres, and San Francisco Giants. Notable former members include the {Link: Atlanta Braves and Houston Astros https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/NL_West_Division}. The division is known for the {Link: rivalry between the Dodgers and Giants https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/NL_West_Division}, and its teams have won multiple World Series titles.

Arizona Diamondbacks

The {Link: Arizona Diamondbacks joined in 1998 https://dodgerblue.com/history-of-the-national-league-and-national-league-west-division/2023/04/15/}, winning the World Series in 2001. They play at {Link: Chase Field https://www.apparelnbags.com/teamcolors/arizona-diamondbacks.htm}. Their original colors represented Arizona's environment and local sports, later changing to reflect the state's landscape. Their rival is the {Link: Los Angeles Dodgers https://www.apparelnbags.com/teamcolors/arizona-diamondbacks.htm}. A key player is {Link: Corbin Carroll https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

Colorado Rockies

The {Link: Colorado Rockies joined in 1993 https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West}. They play at {Link: Coors Field https://www.mlb.com/rockies/ballpark/history}, where some seats are one mile above sea level. Their colors are purple, black, and silver. {Link: They won the NL pennant in 2007 https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/NL_West_Division}. A key player is {Link: Brenton Doyle https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

Los Angeles Dodgers

A founding member of the NL West in 1969, the {Link: Dodgers moved from Brooklyn to Los Angeles in 1958 https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West}. They play at {Link: Dodger Stadium https://www.ballparksofbaseball.com/ballparks/dodger-stadium/}. They are the most successful team in the division, with multiple World Series titles, including in 2020. Their colors are Dodger blue and white. They have a notable rivalry with the {Link: San Francisco Giants https://www.apparelnbags.com/teamcolors/san-francisco-giants.htm}. Key players include {Link: Mookie Betts https://dodgerblue.com/history-of-the-national-league-and-national-league-west-division/2023/04/15/} and {Link: Freddie Freeman https://dodgerblue.com/history-of-the-national-league-and-national-league-west-division/2023/04/15/}.

San Diego Padres

The {Link: San Diego Padres were a founding member in 1969 https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West}. Named after a {Link: Pacific Coast League team https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_San_Diego_Padres}, they moved to {Link: Petco Park https://www.milb.com/news/major-league-affiliate-overview-national-league-west} in 2004. Their distinct brown and gold colors have been part of their identity since the beginning. They won the NL pennant in 1984 and 1998. Key players include {Link: Manny Machado https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team} and {Link: Fernando Tatis Jr https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.

San Francisco Giants

A founding member of the NL West in 1969, the {Link: Giants moved from New York to San Francisco in 1958 https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West}. They play at {Link: Oracle Park https://www.apparelnbags.com/teamcolors/san-francisco-giants.htm}. {Link: Opened in 2000, their ballpark was the first privately financed MLB stadium since Dodger Stadium https://www.mlb.com/giants/history/ballpark}. Their colors are black and orange. They have won multiple World Series titles in San Francisco, including in 2010, 2012, and 2014. Their biggest rival is the {Link: Los Angeles Dodgers https://www.apparelnbags.com/teamcolors/san-francisco-giants.htm}. A key player is {Link: Logan Webb https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on_each_mlb_team}.

The NL West has seen dominance from both the {Link: Los Angeles Dodgers https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West} and the {Link: San Francisco Giants https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West}, and has been home to stars like {Link: Sandy Koufax and Tony Gwynn https://dodgerblue.com/history-of-the-national-league-and-national-league-west-division/2023/04/15/}. The {Link: Diamondbacks also have a World Series title from 2001 https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West}. The {Link: Dodgers and Giants have a long-standing rivalry https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/National_League_West}.
