National League East Teams

{Link: The NL East division was formed in 1969 as part of MLB's expansion https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/NL_East_Division}. It consists of five teams, including the Atlanta Braves, Miami Marlins, New York Mets, Philadelphia Phillies, and Washington Nationals. This division has been home to several strong rivalries, particularly between the Mets and Phillies, and the Braves and Mets.

Atlanta Braves

The {Link: Braves are the oldest continuously operating professional sports franchise in America https://www.mlb.com/braves/history/story-of-the-braves}. The franchise started as the {Link: Boston Red Stockings in the 1870s and later moved to Milwaukee before settling in Atlanta in 1966 https://www.mlb.com/braves/history/story-of-the-braves}. They are the only MLB team to have won a World Series in three different home cities. They play at {Link: Truist Park https://www.mlb.com/braves/history/ballpark}, after previously playing at Atlanta-Fulton County Stadium and Turner Field. Their team colors are red and navy blue, {Link: with blue uniforms being updated with red piping https://shadysportsnetwork.com/atlanta-braves-uniform-history/}. A key player is {Link: Ronald Acuna Jr. https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.

Miami Marlins

The {Link: Marlins joined the NL East in 1993 as an expansion team https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_League_East}. They were originally known as the Florida Marlins and moved to Miami in 2012, becoming the {Link: Miami Marlins https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Miami_Marlins}. They play at {Link: loanDepot Park https://www.mlb.com/marlins/history/ballparks}, having previously played at Sun Life Stadium. Their team colors are blue, black, and white. They have won two World Series championships (1997 and 2003) as a wildcard team.

New York Mets

The {Link: New York Mets were introduced in 1962 as an expansion team, aiming to fill the void left by the Brooklyn Dodgers and New York Giants https://wrrv.com/new-york-mets-origins/}. Their team colors, blue and orange, were chosen to honor these former New York National League teams, {Link: with blue representing the Dodgers and orange representing the Giants https://wrrv.com/new-york-mets-origins/}. They play at {Link: Citi Field https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_New_York_Mets}, whose exterior is inspired by Ebbets Field. A notable rivalry exists with the {Link: Philadelphia Phillies https://www.directv.com/insider/mlb-rivalries/}. {Link: Key players include Juan Soto and Brandon Nimmo https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

Philadelphia Phillies

The {Link: Phillies are one of the National League's charter franchises, dating back to 1883 https://sportsteamhistory.com/mlb-team-history/}. They played at {Link: Baker Bowl https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baker_Bowl} from 1887 until 1938, before moving to {Link: Shibe Park/Connie Mack Stadium https://www.mlb.com/phillies/history/ballparks}. They now play at {Link: Citizens Bank Park https://www.mlb.com/phillies/history/ballparks}. Their team colors are red and white, with blue as a secondary color. Their City Connect jerseys feature blue and yellow, inspired by the city flag. A key player is {Link: Bryce Harper https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.

Washington Nationals

{Link: The Nationals franchise originated as the Montreal Expos, an expansion team in 1969 https://washington.org/visit-dc/washington-nationals-baseball}. They moved to Washington, D.C., in 2005, becoming the {Link: Washington Nationals https://washington.org/visit-dc/washington-nationals-baseball}. They played at RFK Stadium for three seasons before moving to {Link: Nationals Park in 2008 https://washington.org/visit-dc/washington-nationals-baseball}. Their team colors are red and navy blue. Their City Connect jersey features black and purple accents. A key player is {Link: Josiah Gray https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.

{Link: The NL East features several strong rivalries, including the Phillies-Mets rivalry, considered one of the most fierce in the league https://www.directv.com/insider/mlb-rivalries/}. The Braves-Mets rivalry was particularly intense in the late 1990s and early 2000s.
