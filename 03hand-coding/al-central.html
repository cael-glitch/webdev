American League Central Teams

The American League Central Division, often abbreviated AL Central, consists of five teams located in the Midwestern United States. It was established in 1994 as part of a league-wide realignment. At its creation, it included the Chicago White Sox, Cleveland Indians, Kansas City Royals, Milwaukee Brewers, and Minnesota Twins. In 1998, the {Link: Milwaukee Brewers moved to the National League Central, and the Detroit Tigers joined the AL Central from the AL East https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/American_League_Central}. This lineup remains to this day, with the Cleveland team officially changing its name to the {Link: Cleveland Guardians at the end of the 2021 season https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/American_League_Central}. The AL Central is notable as one of two divisions (along with the NL East) where all member teams have won at least one World Series title.

Chicago White Sox

{Link: Founded as the Sioux City Cornhuskers in 1894, the franchise moved to St. Paul in 1895 before settling in Chicago in 1900 as the White Stockings https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}. The name was shortened to {Link: White Sox in 1904 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}. They play at {Link: Guaranteed Rate Field https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/mlb/al-central-ballparks-bm23/}. Their primary team colors are black and white. A notable player is {Link: Tim Anderson https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.

Cleveland Guardians

{Link: The team began as the Cleveland Blues in 1901 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}, later becoming the Naps and then the Indians, before adopting the {Link: Guardians name in 2022 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}. They play at {Link: Progressive Field https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/mlb/al-central-ballparks-bm23/}. Their primary team colors are red and navy blue. A key player is {Link: José Ramírez https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}. The Guardians-Twins rivalry is a notable one within the division.

Detroit Tigers

{Link: Established in 1901 as a charter franchise of the American League, the Detroit Tigers have remained in Detroit since their inception https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}. They play at {Link: Comerica Park https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/mlb/al-central-ballparks-bm23/}. Their primary team colors are navy blue and orange. {Link: They have one of the oldest rivalries in baseball with the Chicago White Sox https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tigers%E2%80%93White_Sox_rivalry}. A significant player for the Tigers is {Link: Riley Greene https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.

Kansas City Royals

{Link: The Kansas City Royals joined the AL as an expansion team in 1969 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}. They won the World Series in 2015. Their home stadium is {Link: Kauffman Stadium https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/mlb/al-central-ballparks-bm23/}. Their primary team colors are royal blue and white, with powder blue as an accent. A key player is {Link: Bobby Witt Jr. https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.

Minnesota Twins

{Link: The Twins franchise began as the Washington Senators in 1901 and relocated to Minnesota in 1961 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}. They play at {Link: Target Field https://www.mlb.com/fans/ballpark-guides}. Their primary team colors are navy blue, red, and white. A notable player is {Link: Byron Buxton https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}. They share a rivalry with the {Link: Cleveland Guardians https://www.mlb.com/news/mlb-best-rivalries-2023}.

The AL Central has seen competitive seasons, with teams like the {Link: Detroit Tigers dominating in the early 2010s https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/AL_Central_Division} and the {Link: Guardians and Twins recently vying for the division title https://www.mlb.com/news/mlb-best-rivalries-2023}. The {Link: Royals also broke through with a World Series victory in 2015 https://www.mlb.com/news/most-indispensable-player-on-each-mlb-team}.
