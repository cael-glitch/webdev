American League West Teams

The American League West division consists of five teams and was created as part of the MLB expansion in 1969. Initially, it had seven members, including the {Link: Chicago White Sox, Kansas City Royals, and Minnesota Twins, which all moved to the AL Central during the 1994 realignment https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/AL_West_Division}. {Link: The Milwaukee Brewers, originally the Seattle Pilots, moved to the AL East in 1972 before eventually joining the NL Central https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/AL_West_Division}. {Link: The Houston Astros joined the AL West in 2013 after playing in the National League https://baseball.fandom.com/wiki/American_League_West}. The division features teams located along the West Coast and in Texas.

Houston Astros

The {Link: Houston Astros started as the Colt .45s in 1962 and became the Astros in 1965 when they moved into the Astrodome https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/houston-astros}. They joined the AL West in 2013. They play at {Link: Minute Maid Park https://www.mlb.com/astros/team/jobs/our-history}. Their team colors are navy blue and orange. Notable players include {Link: José Altuve and Yordan Álvarez https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

Los Angeles Angels

The {Link: Angels were established in 1961 as one of the league's original expansion teams https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/los-angeles-angels}. They have gone by several names, including the California Angels and Anaheim Angels, before becoming the {Link: Los Angeles Angels https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels}. They play at {Link: Angel Stadium https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/mlb/al-west-ballparks-bm23/}. Their team colors are red and white. {Link: Notable players include Mike Trout and Zach Neto https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

Oakland Athletics

{Link: The Athletics began in Philadelphia in 1901, moved to Kansas City in 1955, and then to Oakland in 1968 https://www.retroseasons.com/teams/oakland-athletics/history/logos/}. {Link: They are known for the "Moneyball" era https://www.britannica.com/topic/Oakland-Athletics}. Their team colors are green and gold. The team has won nine World Series championships and 15 American League pennants. {Link: Oakland has had the most MVPs in the division https://www.action247.com/sport/mlb/american-league/al-west/}. Notable players include {Link: Brent Rooker and Lawrence Butler https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

Seattle Mariners

{Link: The Mariners were established in 1977 as an expansion team https://www.britannica.com/topic/Seattle-Mariners}. Their home ballpark is {Link: T-Mobile Park https://www.mlb.com/news/major-league-affiliate-overview-american-league-west}. They are the only team in MLB to have never played in the World Series. Their team colors are navy blue, metallic silver, and Northwest green. Notable players include {Link: Julio Rodríguez and J. P. Crawford https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

Texas Rangers

{Link: The Texas Rangers started as the Washington Senators in 1961 and moved to Texas in 1972 https://www.baseball-reference.com/bullpen/AL_West_Division}. They recently won their first World Series in 2023. They play at {Link: Globe Life Field https://www.milb.com/news/major-league-affiliate-overview-american-league-west}. Their primary team colors are blue, red, and white. Notable players include {Link: Corey Seager and Wyatt Langford https://www.justbaseball.com/mlb/al-west-power-rankings-preview-2025/}.

{Link: The AL West has produced several MVP winners, including Shohei Ohtani of the Angels in 2021 https://www.action247.com/sport/mlb/american-league/al-west/}. {Link: The division has also featured intense rivalries, such as the Angels-Athletics and Astros-Rangers matchups https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=HSeRosaiMy8}.
