American League East Teams

The {Link: American League East division was created in 1969 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League_East} as Major League Baseball expanded and reorganized its leagues into divisions. Initially, it included six teams: the Baltimore Orioles, Boston Red Sox, Cleveland Indians, Detroit Tigers, New York Yankees, and Washington Senators. After the Senators moved in 1971 and became the Texas Rangers in the AL West, the Milwaukee Brewers moved into the AL East to maintain balance. The Toronto Blue Jays joined as an expansion team in 1977. When MLB realigned into three divisions in 1994, the Cleveland Indians and Detroit Tigers moved to the AL Central. The Tampa Bay Devil Rays were added as an expansion team in 1998, with their name changing to the {Link: Tampa Bay Rays in 2008 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League_East}. This division is often considered one of the toughest in baseball.

Baltimore Orioles

The {Link: Baltimore Orioles franchise dates back to 1901 as the Milwaukee Brewers https://sportsteamhistory.com/boston-red-sox/}. They moved to St. Louis in 1902, becoming the St. Louis Browns, and relocated to Baltimore in 1954, adopting the Orioles nickname. They play at {Link: Oriole Park at Camden Yards https://www.retroseasons.com/teams/baltimore-orioles/}, known for its influence on modern ballpark design. The team's colors are orange and black. Key players include {Link: Brooks Robinson, Frank Robinson, Jim Palmer, Eddie Murray, and Cal Ripken Jr. https://www.retroseasons.com/teams/baltimore-orioles/}, among other greats.

Boston Red Sox

The {Link: Boston Red Sox were founded in 1901 as the Boston Americans and adopted the Red Sox name in 1908 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}. They play at {Link: Fenway Park https://sportsteamhistory.com/boston-red-sox/}, which opened in 1912. The Red Sox's primary team colors are red and navy blue. Their history includes the "Curse of the Bambino," which ended with a World Series win in 2004, and several other championships. Notable players include {Link: Ted Williams, Carl Yastrzemski, and David Ortiz https://sportsteamhistory.com/boston-red-sox/}.

New York Yankees

The {Link: New York Yankees were established in 1903 as the New York Highlanders, replacing the Baltimore Orioles https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League}, and later became known as the Yankees. They are famous for their navy blue and white pinstripes and have won more World Series championships than any other MLB team. The Yankees' main rivals are the Boston Red Sox. Key players include {Link: Babe Ruth, Lou Gehrig, Joe DiMaggio, Mickey Mantle, and Derek Jeter https://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/the-50-greatest-players-in-new-york-yankees-history-robert-w-cohen/1108040320?ean=9781589798151}. The Yankees played in the first World Series in 1903, losing to the Boston Red Sox.

Tampa Bay Rays

The {Link: Tampa Bay Rays joined the AL East as an expansion team in 1998, originally known as the Tampa Bay Devil Rays https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League_East}. They changed their name to the Rays in 2008. Their primary team colors are navy blue and light blue. They are known for their strong performance in recent years, despite a smaller market. Key players include {Link: Carl Crawford, Evan Longoria, and David Price https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VZCrJD5i5zE}.

Toronto Blue Jays

The {Link: Toronto Blue Jays joined the AL East as an expansion team in 1977 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League_East}. Their team colors are blue and white, with a darker blue accent. They are the only Canadian MLB team and won back-to-back World Series titles in 1992 and 1993. Key players include {Link: Roberto Alomar, Carlos Delgado, Roy Halladay, and Dave Stieb https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-5g77xnetGs}. As of September 4, 2025, the {Link: Blue Jays have a magic number of 20 to clinch the AL East https://www.si.com/mlb/bluejays/news/blue-jays-al-east-magic-number-gets-boost-blowing-out-reds}.

{Link: The AL East has sent a team to the World Series 28 times and produced 16 World Series champions https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League_East}. Since the introduction of the Wild Card in 1995, the AL East has produced 28 Wild Card teams.
