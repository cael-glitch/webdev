National League Central Teams

The {Link: National League Central division https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_League_Central} was created in 1994 as part of a league-wide realignment. It was originally composed of five teams from the NL West (Reds, Astros) and NL East (Cubs, Pirates, Cardinals). The Milwaukee Brewers joined the NL Central in 1998, moving from the AL Central. Houston left for the AL West in 2013. The division is known for strong rivalries, including the Cubs-Cardinals and Brewers-Cubs matchups.

Chicago Cubs

The {Link: Cubs are a charter member of the National League, founded in 1876 as the White Stockings https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/sports-and-leisure/chicago-cubs}. They have played in Wrigley Field since 1916. Their team colors are royal blue, red, and white. They famously broke a 108-year World Series drought in 2016. Notable players include {Link: Ernie Banks, Ron Santo, Ryne Sandberg, and Anthony Rizzo https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/sports-and-leisure/chicago-cubs}.

Cincinnati Reds

The {Link: Cincinnati Reds' history connects to the 1869 Red Stockings, baseball's first professional team https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/sports-and-leisure/cincinnati-reds}. They have won five World Series titles, including back-to-back championships in 1975 and 1976 with the "Big Red Machine." They play at {Link: Great American Ball Park https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/mlb/nl-central-ballparks-bm23/}. Their colors are red and white. Notable players include {Link: Johnny Bench, Joe Morgan, and Pete Rose https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/sports-and-leisure/cincinnati-reds}.

Milwaukee Brewers

The {Link: Brewers began in 1969 as the Seattle Pilots and moved to Milwaukee for the 1970 season https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/milwaukee-brewers}. They have played in the AL West, AL East, and AL Central before joining the NL Central in 1998. They play at {Link: American Family Field https://sports.betmgm.com/en/blog/mlb/nl-central-ballparks-bm23/}. Their colors are navy blue, yellow, and royal blue. Key players include {Link: Robin Yount, Paul Molitor, and Christian Yelich https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/milwaukee-brewers}.

Pittsburgh Pirates

The {Link: Pirates, who joined the National League in 1887 https://www.mlb.com/pirates/history/timeline/early-years}, have won five World Series titles. Their home stadium is {Link: PNC Park https://positivelypittsburgh.com/baseballhistory/}. Since 1948, their colors have been black, gold, and white, derived from the flag of Pittsburgh. Notable players include {Link: Roberto Clemente, Honus Wagner, and Willie Stargell https://positivelypittsburgh.com/baseballhistory/}.

St. Louis Cardinals

The {Link: Cardinals were established in 1882 https://www.britannica.com/topic/St-Louis-Cardinals} and have won 11 World Series titles, second only to the New York Yankees. They moved to the National League in 1892. Their home stadium is {Link: Busch Stadium https://www.britannica.com/topic/St-Louis-Cardinals}. Their colors are cardinal red and navy blue. Key players include {Link: Stan Musial, Bob Gibson, and Albert Pujols https://www.britannica.com/topic/St-Louis-Cardinals}.

{Link: The NL Central has produced strong rivalries, including the Cubs-Cardinals "Route 66 rivalry" https://blog.ticketmaster.com/biggest-mlb-rivalries/}, one of the oldest and most intense in baseball. The {Link: Brewers-Cubs rivalry is also fierce https://blog.ticketmaster.com/biggest-mlb-rivalries/}. The division has seen its share of success and intense competition since its formation in 1994.
