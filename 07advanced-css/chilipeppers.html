The Tri-City Chili Peppers

The Tri-City Chili Peppers are a collegiate summer baseball team based in Colonial Heights, Virginia. They play their home games at Shepherd Stadium and are a member of the Coastal Plain League (CPL). The team is also widely known for its unique "Cosmic Baseball" events, featuring glow-in-the-dark gear and a high-energy atmosphere.

History and Cosmic Baseball

The Chili Peppers joined the Coastal Plain League in 2020 but had their inaugural season delayed until 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Their first home game on May 31, 2021, was a 10-8 victory over the Wilson Tobs. The team gained national attention in 2024 for creating "Cosmic Baseball," where games are played under black lights with glow-in-the-dark equipment and uniforms. The success of this event led to the creation of the 2025 Cosmic Takeover Tour, bringing the unique spectacle to ballparks across the country.

In 2024, the Coastal Plain League named the Tri-City Chili Peppers its Organization of the Year, recognizing the team's creative approach to fan entertainment and community engagement. The "Cosmic Baseball" concept has been featured on major media outlets such as ESPN, MLB Network, and CNN.

Notable Alumni and Tour Success

As a member of the Coastal Plain League, the Chili Peppers are part of a league that has produced many Major League Baseball players and even a Super Bowl champion quarterback. Notable alumni from the CPL include Justin Verlander, Russell Wilson, Ryan Zimmerman, and Alec Bohm. The Chili Peppers' own success with Cosmic Baseball has led to a major partnership with sportswear brand 3N2 for their custom gear.

The 2025 Cosmic Takeover Tour has been a major triumph, selling out games and creating a buzz on social media. The Chili Peppers led the tour in overall games with a 10-6 record and recently set a Cosmic Baseball record by scoring 38 runs and hitting 38 home runs in a single game in Toledo, Ohio. The team's roster for the tour features professional players and college standouts, including Marcus VanAlstine and Zion Taylor, who return for the cosmic experience.

Mascot and Community

The team's mascot is Blaze, a hot pepper who engages with fans and adds to the game-day fun. The Chili Peppers organization is known for its strong community focus, with owner Chris Martin expressing a passion for bringing baseball and entertainment to the area. The team's commitment to community was highlighted by the overwhelming response from fans during the planning of the Cosmic Takeover Tour.
